CIA TRIAD RESEARCH

1. CONFIDENTIALITY

Definition:
Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized individuals and protected from unauthorized access.

Why It Is Important:
Confidentiality protects personal, financial, academic, and organizational data from disclosure to unauthorized users.

Real-World Example:
A university stores student records in a secure database that can only be accessed by authorized staff members.

---

2. INTEGRITY

Definition:
Integrity ensures that information remains accurate, complete, and unaltered unless modified by authorized individuals.

Why It Is Important:
Integrity maintains trust in data and prevents unauthorized changes that could lead to incorrect decisions.

Real-World Example:
Examination results stored in a college database must not be modified by unauthorized users.

---

3. AVAILABILITY

Definition:
Availability ensures that systems, applications, and data are accessible whenever authorized users need them.

Why It Is Important:
Organizations depend on continuous access to services and information for daily operations.

Real-World Example:
A college website should remain accessible to students during examination registration periods.

---

| Component       | Purpose                                    | Example             |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------- |
| Confidentiality | Protect data from unauthorized access      | Student records     |
| Integrity       | Maintain data accuracy and trustworthiness | Examination results |
| Availability    | Ensure systems remain accessible           | College website     |
