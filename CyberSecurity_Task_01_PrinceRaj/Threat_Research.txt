CYBER THREAT RESEARCH

1. PHISHING

Definition:
Phishing is a cyberattack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information through fake emails, websites, or messages.

Real-World Example:
Attackers send fake banking emails asking users to verify account information.

Prevention:

* Verify sender identity.
* Avoid clicking suspicious links.
* Use MFA.
* Check website URLs carefully.

---

2. MALWARE

Definition:
Malware is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

Real-World Example:
The WannaCry malware infected thousands of computers worldwide.

Prevention:

* Use antivirus software.
* Keep systems updated.
* Avoid downloading files from untrusted sources.

---

3. RANSOMWARE

Definition:
Ransomware encrypts files and demands payment to restore access.

Real-World Example:
The Colonial Pipeline ransomware attack disrupted fuel distribution operations.

Prevention:

* Maintain regular backups.
* Keep software updated.
* Avoid suspicious attachments and downloads.

---

4. SOCIAL ENGINEERING

Definition:
Social engineering manipulates people into revealing confidential information.

Real-World Example:
An attacker impersonates an IT support employee and requests login credentials.

Prevention:

* Verify identities before sharing information.
* Follow security procedures.
* Participate in security awareness training.

---

5. DATA BREACH

Definition:
A data breach occurs when sensitive information is accessed without authorization.

Real-World Example:
The Equifax data breach exposed personal information of millions of users.

Prevention:

* Use strong passwords.
* Enable MFA.
* Encrypt sensitive information.
* Monitor systems regularly.
